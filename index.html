Neural Network-Based Obstavle Aware Trajectory Generation for UAVs

I developed a neural-network-based framework for collision-free trajectory generation for quadcopters operating in static environments. A Holybro X-500 V2 quadcopter equipped with a Pixhawk 6C flight controller, Jetson Nano, and RealSense D435 stereo camera was used for real time implementation. YOLOv3 Tiny (integrated via a ROS wrapper) and the stereocamera were used to perform object detection and localization (ODL). Using optimal trajectories obtained using successive convex optimization, I trained three neural networks—NN-Trajectory, NN-Parameter, and NN-Segment—to approximate and reconstruct optimal paths, with Optuna used for hyperparameter tuning and architecture optimization. To enhance safety, I introduced a safety margin correction step to dynamically adjust position-level information near obstacles, ensuring collision-free trajectories with minimal accuracy loss. Additionally, I developed a monocular camera-based human localization algorithm that accurately estimates relative position, velocity, and associated uncertainty without relying on computationally expensive depth estimation methods, making it ideal for embedded and resource-constrained platforms. Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of the framework in multiple scenarios, with position estimates comparable to existing methods and velocity estimates capturing overall trends, though further improvements are needed for accuracy.

Speed and Phase Synchronizing Controllers for Multi-Rotors

The phase offset between propellers affect the noise and vibrations in aerial vehicles that use multi-rotor systems. In this work, I developed and validated a phase controller for multi-rotor systems. I tested the proposed phase control algorithm across various rotor configurations, including a single rotor, side-by-side rotors, and co-rotating co-axial rotors. The literature suggests that inter-rotor spacing in co-axial rotors significantly influences rotor-rotor interactions, which in turn affects the performance of phase control. To assess the robustness of the controller, I tested the algorithm at three inter-rotor spacings corresponding to different aerodynamic interaction regimes. To evaluate the effectiveness of the phase controller, I developed a post-validation algorithm using a high-speed imaging system coupled with MATLAB’s image processing toolbox. The experimental results demonstrate that the controller maintains phase angle changes within 1° for up to 100 revolutions across all tested rotor configurations.

Keywords: LabVIEW, Arduino, Python, MATLAB, PID Control, High Speed Imaging.

Uncertainty Aware Optimal Control

Nonlinear optimal control problems (OCPs) often exhibit dispersion under model uncertainties, making it crucial to generate optimal solutions that are less sensitive to these uncertainties. In this work, I propose a novel solution desensitization method, the Reduced Desensitization Formulation (RDF), by leveraging the non-uniqueness of the solution of the costate differential equations when a hybrid indirect-direct method is used. A key advantage of the RDF method is its ability to significantly reduce the number of differential equations required for generating desensitized solutions. This feature enables the efficient computation of open-loop desensitized trajectories, making the approach applicable to OCPs with a larger number of uncertain parameters. To demonstrate the effectiveness of the RDF method, I applied it to three important classes of trajectory optimization problems, all of which include uncertainty in the thrust magnitude of the propulsion system: 1) minimum-fuel low-thrust rendezvous maneuvers, 2) low-thrust orbit-raising maneuvers, and 3) minimum-fuel high-thrust rocket-landing problems. For these problems, which involve bang-bang control profiles, I analyzed the change in the number of control switches between standard and desensitized optimal solutions. The numerical results confirm that the RDF method effectively desensitizes the considered performance indices with respect to thrust magnitude. Additionally, I introduce a time-triggered desensitization mechanism by modifying the RDF method. Instead of desensitizing the entire trajectory, I selectively apply desensitization to specific segments, thereby improving the optimality of the desensitized trajectories while maintaining robustness to model uncertainties.

Keywords: Non-Linear Optimization, Optimal Control Theory, Space Trajectory Optimization.
