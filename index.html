Neural Network-Based Obstavle Aware Trajectory Generation for UAVs

A Holybro X-500 V2 quadcopter platform equipped with a Pixhawk 6c flight controller is used for all data collection and algorithm evaluation. A Jetson Nano acts as the companion computer, while a RealSense D435 stereo camera is used for object detection and localization (ODL). Position and orientation estimates are obtained from an OptiTrack PrimeX 22 motion capture (MOCAP) system, which provides state estimation data to the Pixhawk. For perception, YOLOv3 Tiny was employed for object detection and is integrated via a ROS wrapper. The combination of the RealSense D435 and YOLOv3 Tiny enables ODL. For guidance, a neural-network-based framework was developed to facilitate quadcopter navigation in environments with static obstacles. Trajectory generation was achieved using a successive convexification method, and three neural network (NN) models were designed to approximate and reconstruct optimal paths: 1. NN-Trajectory: Predicts the full trajectory. 2. NN-Parameter: Predicts critical infomation near obstacles . 3. NN-Segment: Predicts segment wise trajectory. To enhance safety, a safety margin correction step was introduced, dynamically adjusting the position of parameter points (those closest to obstacles). This ensures collision-free trajectories with minimal loss of accuracy.
